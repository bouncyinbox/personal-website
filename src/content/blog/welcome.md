This is the start of an occasional notebook on engineering management.

I expect to write about a narrow set of things:

- The mechanics of running teams that ship — planning, reviews, hiring, the boring stuff that compounds.
- Specific decisions I've made (and unmade), with enough context that the lesson travels.
- Tools and practices I've actually adopted, not ones I've only read about.

What you won't find here:

- Generic advice that could have been written by anyone.
- Hot takes on the week's tech news.
- Anything that pretends management is simpler than it is.

If a post here saves one manager from one bad week, that's the bar.
